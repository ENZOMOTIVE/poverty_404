# Doctors for Madagascar
Doctors for Madagascar collects and uses different types of monitoring and evaluation data, for example project indicators, TB cohort data, DHIS2-related health service data and REDCap exports. These data contain highly relevant information for project management, reporting, quality improvement and research. However, turning raw data into actionable insights is still time-consuming and requires technical and contextual expertise.
Project managers and M&E staff often need to manually check data quality, interpret trends, identify gaps in service delivery, prepare explanations for project teams and translate findings into understandable reports or presentations. This is especially challenging when data are complex, fragmented across systems, or need to be interpreted in line with health standards such as WHO guidance.
The challenge is to develop an AI-supported M&E Data Assistant that helps Doctors for Madagascar make better use of existing health and project data.
The assistant could support three connected functions:
Data quality checking
The assistant could review structured data exports, for example from REDCap, or similar M&E datasets and detect inconsistencies, missing values, implausible entries or reporting errors. Instead of only flagging errors, it should explain in understandable language, ideally in French, why the issue matters, how it affects data quality and how it could be reviewed or corrected. This would support more reliable monitoring, research and reporting across different projects.
Healthcare gap and risk exploration
The AI assistant could help explore links between health service utilisation data, for example DHIS2-related data, and external contextual data such as climate, seasonality or accessibility-related information. The goal would be to identify possible seasonal risk windows or patterns associated with healthcare gaps, for example periods or areas where healthcare utilisation may decline or service needs may increase. This could support anticipatory planning, project steering and future research on the relationship between climate, seasonality and healthcare utilisation.
Insight generation, situation explanation and impact storytelling
The AI assistant could transform selected M&E indicators or health data into concise summaries, visualisation suggestions and impact stories for different audiences, such as project teams, donors or partners. This could also include a specific project-related use case: project coordinators could ask questions in French about existing data, and the AI assistant would provide clear, context-aware and WHO-compliant explanations of the current situation in a facility or project area. The tool should help identify what has changed, where attention is needed and what story the data can tell.
The goal is to build a practical support tool for data quality, data interpretation, reporting, research and internal learning.
